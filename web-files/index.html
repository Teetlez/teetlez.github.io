About Me

Background

I grew up in a multicultural household, a South African mother and an American father. This experience has given me a unique perspective and appreciation for different cultures and ways of life. After graduating from high school, I knew I wanted to pursue a career in technology and decided to attend the University of New Hampshire to study Computer Science. During my studies, I discovered a passion for programming and the thrill of solving complex problems. I am a detail-oriented individual taking pride in my work and looking to improve my skills and knowledge

Interests

Ever since I discovered the world of programming, I have been captivated by the ability to create systems that adapt to changes and tell complex stories from simple inputs. This fascination led me to the study of computer graphics, where I became enamored with the techniques used to create stunning visuals. Before I had access to a computer with a decent video card, I spent countless hours watching YouTube videos of games and 3D-rendered animations, studying and admiring the graphical techniques used, such as shadow maps, bump mapping, parallax textures, global illumination, ambient occlusion, volumetric lighting, and more. The allure of creating art through logic was inescapable, and I have been in love with it ever since. This passion has only grown stronger over the years, and I continue to explore new techniques and algorithims to grow my knowledge. In my free time, I enjoy a wide range of other activities, including snowboarding, philosophy, 3D-printing, video games, airsoft, and chess.

Work Experience

Intern Software Developer / UNH Interopability Lab
Jan 2019 - Sep 2022 (~4 years)

I worked with different vendors to validate their products' conformance to IETF standards. More specifically, I helped design, maintain, and run testing packages for standards such as IPv6, DHCPv6, IPsec, CE Router, NPP, BGP, IKEv2, and OSPFv3. I also helped maintain the large-scale software called INTACTTM, which ran the packages. All this was done within an Agile team structure.

In addition to this, I participated in multiple in-house projects to either improve our already-made proprietary tools or create new ones to automate and improve the quality of work. Overall, this has given me about 3-4 years of industry-level software development experience.

VR Developer / Albacore Museum
Aug 2021 - May 2022 (~1 year)

During my senior year, I (along with three other undergrads) worked with the Albacore Museum to develop a virtual reality app for the Oculus Quest 2. This was done with the Unity game engine and Blender 3D modeling software. The app was designed to be an interactive, virtual environment similar in appearance to the USS Albacore submarine.

Some of my responsibilities included repository management, app deployment, optimization, engine scripting, level and art design, testing, documentation, sound design, and modeling. Our product won 1st place in the UNH 2022 Undergraduate Research Conference for Applications of Computer Science.

Volunteer Teacher / Mamelodi Initiative
Jun 2022

For about a month, I worked in Mamelodi, South Africa, as a volunteer teacher for the 2022 Winter Jam program. The program was designed to help students, during their winter break, perform at or above grade level in their subjects by focusing on their math and English. In the morning, I assisted in teaching the students basic mathematics, such as addition, multiplication, division, and fractions. In the afternoon, I led a coding workshop, teaching students about programming and computer science.
